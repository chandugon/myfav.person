My Favourite People




Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, whose full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, stands as one of India’s most revered scientists and leaders. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble family, he overcame early financial hardships through determination and hard work. Kalam’s academic journey began at St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli, where he studied Physics, followed by Aeronautical Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.

Kalam’s career began at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958, and he later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). At ISRO, he played a pivotal role in developing India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-3), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980. His leadership in missile technology, particularly with the Agni and Prithvi missiles, earned him the title “Missile Man of India.” He was also instrumental in advancing India’s nuclear capabilities, notably during the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was loved by all generations for his wisdom, humility, and vision for a developed India. His life continues to inspire millions of young Indians to dream big and serve the nation selflessly.





Rohit Gurunath Sharma

Rohit Gurunath Sharma

Rohit Gurunath Sharma, born on April 30, 1987, in Bansod, Nagpur, Maharashtra, is one of India’s most accomplished cricketers and the current captain of the Indian men's national cricket team in all formats. Known for his elegant batting style and powerful stroke play, Rohit holds the record for the highest individual score in a One Day International (ODI) — an astonishing 264 runs against Sri Lanka in 2014.

Rohit began his international career in 2007 and quickly rose to prominence with his consistency and leadership skills. He has scored multiple double centuries in ODIs and is also the only player to have scored three double centuries in this format. As an opening batsman, he has been a key figure in India’s limited-overs dominance.

Under his captaincy, the Mumbai Indians (MI) franchise has won five Indian Premier League (IPL) titles, making him one of the most successful captains in IPL history. Known as the "Hitman" of Indian cricket, Rohit Sharma is admired for his calm demeanor, sharp cricketing acumen, and ability to perform under pressure.



Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar, born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, Maharashtra, is widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers in the history of the sport. Known as the "Master Blaster" and "Little Master," Sachin made his debut for India at the young age of 16 and went on to have a remarkable career spanning over two decades.

Tendulkar holds numerous records, including being the highest run-scorer in the history of Test and One Day International (ODI) cricket. He is the first player to score 100 international centuries and the first to score a double century in ODIs. His elegant batting style, unmatched consistency, and humility earned him admiration from fans and fellow players worldwide.

Sachin was a key figure in India's 2011 World Cup victory and was awarded the Bharat Ratna — India’s highest civilian award — in 2014, becoming the youngest recipient. Even after retirement, he continues to inspire millions and remains a symbol of dedication, discipline, and excellence in Indian sports.



Khan Sir

Khan Sir

Khan Sir, widely known for his charismatic teaching style and deep knowledge of General Studies, is a popular Indian educator based in Patna, Bihar. His real name is reportedly Faizal Khan, though he is most famously referred to by his students as "Khan Sir." He rose to national prominence through his YouTube channel “Khan GS Research Centre,” where he simplifies complex topics with humor, real-life examples, and regional language expressions.

Coming from a modest background, Khan Sir's rise to fame reflects his dedication to education and his unique ability to connect with students from rural and semi-urban areas. His mission is to make quality education accessible and affordable for all, especially for competitive exams like UPSC, SSC, and state-level exams.

Khan Sir's authenticity, patriotic tone, and strong command over current affairs have earned him millions of followers. Despite controversies and media attention, he remains committed to his goal of empowering the youth of India through education.



Ratan Tata

Ratan Tata

Ratan Naval Tata, born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai, is one of India’s most respected industrialists and philanthropists. As the former chairman of Tata Group, he transformed the organization into a global business powerhouse while upholding high standards of ethics, simplicity, and social responsibility.

A graduate of Cornell University in architecture and Harvard Business School’s Advanced Management Program, Ratan Tata joined the Tata Group in 1962. Under his leadership, the group expanded globally with landmark acquisitions such as Tetley, Corus, Jaguar, and Land Rover. His vision led to the development of the Tata Nano, the world’s cheapest car, aimed at making car ownership accessible to all.

Despite his corporate stature, Ratan Tata is deeply committed to philanthropy and social causes. A majority of Tata Sons' profits are directed toward education, healthcare, and rural development. He is a recipient of the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan and continues to inspire millions with his humility, wisdom, and relentless pursuit of innovation.
